Terms Definitions
Anarchism An ideology seeking the replacement of political and state authority with a self-regulated society of individuals and freely formed groups.
Authority A group in society which has the social position of power (not to be confused with state).
Bias A prejudicial notion that influences one’s interpretation of information they are presented with, usually in a way concurrent with one’s upbringing.
Bourgeoisie The class of people in society that own the means of production as private property.
Bureaucracy A part of society who has control of another part of society and by nature of their position in society have interests different from those they administer.
Capitalism An ideology where social relations are based on the idea of commodities for exchange, particularly private ownership of the means of production and exploitation of wage labour.
Centralization The process of concentrating decision-making to a single point.
Centrism A political outlook that rejects views deviating from the status quo.
Censorship Active suppression of information.
Class A group of people sharing common relations to labor and the means of production.
Commodity Something that is produced for the sole purpose of trading it for something else.
Communism An ideology that aims to put the workers in power and create a classless, stateless, and moneyless society.
Credit An accentuation of capitalist antagonisms, it is the means by which expansion may be pushed to its absolute limits.
Decentralization The process of distributing activity as far as possible.
